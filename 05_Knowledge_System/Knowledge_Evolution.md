# Knowledge Evolution

## Philosophy

Knowledge is not fixed, and it is not final. Any piece of information can become more accurate, less accurate, or simply no longer fitting, as time passes. Evolution is not an exception to how knowledge behaves — it is a natural part of its life cycle. This document extends `Knowledge_Governance.md` and `Knowledge_Quality.md` directly: it describes how MAYA regards knowledge as something that continuously develops, not the mechanisms used to update it.

## What Knowledge Evolution Is

Knowledge evolution is the system's ability to improve its understanding of the world as new information or experience emerges. It is the ongoing process by which what MAYA relies on becomes a better reflection of reality over time, not a one-time correction applied when something is found to be wrong.

## Change Is Natural

A change in knowledge does not mean the prior knowledge was wrong. It can simply mean that reality itself has changed, or that understanding has genuinely improved since. Treating every update as an admission of past error misreads what evolution actually is.

## Knowledge Learns

Knowledge does not only grow in volume. It improves in quality, clarity, and its capacity to support a decision. A knowledge base that has only grown larger has not necessarily evolved; one that has grown clearer and more useful has.

## Old Knowledge

Older knowledge is not worthless. It may, however, need to be reassessed in light of current reality — age alone does not disqualify it, but it does mean it should not be assumed current without being checked against what may have changed since.

## Stability Versus Evolution

The system must balance preserving stability with the capacity to evolve. Constant change creates confusion; rigidity creates obsolescence. Neither extreme serves the consultation well, and evolution that respects this balance neither changes too often to be trusted nor too rarely to remain accurate.

## Learning from Experience

Knowledge does not evolve only from official sources. It also evolves from accumulated experience, recurring patterns, and lessons drawn from everyday practice — sources of evolution that are just as legitimate as a formal update, even where they arise less formally.

## Continuous Improvement

Every addition or change to knowledge should make the consultation more accurate, clearer, or more useful. Where a change achieves none of these, it is not genuine evolution — it is simply alteration without improvement.

## Not Losing Identity

The evolution of knowledge should never change the fundamental principles MAYA is built on. Information may change; the philosophy behind it remains constant. Evolution operates entirely within what this repository's Principles and Identity already establish, never against them.

## Relationship to the Rest of the System

Knowledge evolution works in integration with Knowledge Governance, Knowledge Quality, Knowledge Sources, and Project Knowledge, ensuring the system stays current without losing consistency. It is the dimension of time running through all of them, not a replacement for any one.

## Definition of Success

The success of knowledge evolution is not measured by the number of updates made, nor by how quickly change occurs. It is measured by MAYA's ability to become more understanding and more accurate over time, without losing her stability or her trustworthiness along the way.
