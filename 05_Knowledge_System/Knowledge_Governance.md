# Knowledge Governance

## Philosophy

Knowledge is not merely content. It is a strategic asset that every one of MAYA's decisions depends on. For this reason, knowledge must never change arbitrarily, and it must never exist without clear responsibility attached to it. Governance exists to protect the quality of knowledge — not to slow its evolution. This document extends `Knowledge_Model.md`, `Knowledge_Quality.md`, and `Knowledge_Sources.md` directly: it defines the principles that keep knowledge trustworthy over time, not the procedures used to manage or publish it.

## What Knowledge Governance Is

Knowledge governance is the set of principles that organize how knowledge is adopted and how its trustworthiness is preserved as time passes. It exists wherever knowledge is relied on repeatedly, regardless of how that knowledge happens to be recorded or distributed.

## Knowledge Has Responsibility

Every piece of knowledge should have a party responsible for its correctness and for how long it remains valid. This is not about identifying individuals for their own sake — it is about ensuring that no piece of knowledge exists without an owner accountable for it remaining trustworthy.

## Formal Adoption

Not every piece of information becomes part of MAYA's knowledge automatically. Official knowledge is knowledge that has been adopted according to governing principles — something being true or plausible is not, by itself, sufficient for it to be treated as part of what MAYA relies on.

## Disciplined Change

Updating knowledge is normal and expected. But it must happen in a way that preserves consistency, and does not introduce contradictions or confusion into MAYA's decisions. Change that bypasses this discipline is not an update — it is a source of the very inconsistency governance exists to prevent.

## Reviewability

Any knowledge used in a consultation should be reviewable when needed. This is not for the purpose of assigning blame — it is for the purpose of improvement and learning, and knowledge that cannot be traced back and reviewed cannot be genuinely trusted, however confident it appears.

## Consistency Across the System

Knowledge should not conflict between one project and another, between one document and another, or between one policy and another. Where a conflict arises, it must be addressed plainly rather than left unresolved or absorbed silently into whichever version happens to be used first.

## Continuous Evolution

Governance does not prevent change. It makes change safe and orderly — a governed system is not a static one; it is one that can keep evolving without losing the trust it has already earned.

## Trust

The ultimate purpose of governance is to build trust: the customer's trust in the consultation they receive, the team's trust in the knowledge behind it, and MAYA's own trust in the decisions she builds from it. Where any one of these is missing, governance has not yet done its job, regardless of how well-organized the underlying content may appear.

## Relationship to the Rest of the System

Knowledge governance operates in integration with Knowledge Sources, Knowledge Quality, Project Knowledge, and the Memory Model. It does not replace any of them — it is the layer that keeps all of them coherent with one another as they each continue to evolve.

## Definition of Success

The success of knowledge governance is not measured by the number of rules it contains, nor by the number of documents behind it. It is measured by the system's ability to keep evolving while preserving the trust, consistency, and quality of the knowledge MAYA relies on.
