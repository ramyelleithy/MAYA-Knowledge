# Project Knowledge

## Philosophy

A project is not a price. It is not a brochure, and it is not a PDF file. For MAYA to genuinely know a project, she must know it the way the best senior property consultant in the company would know it. That knowledge must be sufficient to support a decision — not merely sufficient to be recalled. This document extends `Project_Brain.md` directly: where that document establishes what a Project Brain is, this one defines the specific categories of knowledge a Project Brain must actually hold to function.

## Project Knowledge Defined

Project knowledge is the complete set of facts, conclusions, and context related to a project that genuinely helps MAYA reach a correct recommendation. Knowledge that does not serve a decision about this project has not earned a place in this category, regardless of how thoroughly documented it may be elsewhere.

## Core Identity

Every project has a clear identity: its developer, its name, its type, its stage of development, its location, and its general vision. This identity establishes what the project is. It is necessary, but on its own it is not sufficient to support a consultation — knowing what a project is called and where it sits does not yet tell MAYA whether or for whom it is the right recommendation.

## Commercial Knowledge

MAYA must understand a project's commercial dimensions: the segments of customer it genuinely targets, its competitive strengths, its weaknesses, the value it actually proposes, the reasons a customer would choose it, and the reasons a customer would not. This is the layer that begins to connect a project's identity to actual customer decisions.

## Operational Knowledge

MAYA must know the information that bears on a present decision: prices, payment plans, available units, delivery timing, current offers, and recent updates. This knowledge is understood, from the outset, to be temporary by nature — subject to change without warning, and never treated as though it were as durable as a project's identity or commercial positioning.

## Comparative Knowledge

MAYA does not know a project in isolation. She knows its alternatives, its competitors, when it genuinely outperforms them, and when it is not the best choice available. A project's standing is only meaningful relative to what else exists around it, and comparative knowledge is what makes that standing legible.

## Consultative Knowledge

The most important knowledge about a project is not its facts. It is knowing whom the project genuinely suits, whom it does not, and under which circumstances it becomes the right choice or the wrong one. This is the layer that turns everything else — identity, commercial positioning, operational detail, and comparison — into something usable for an actual recommendation.

## Changing and Stable Knowledge

Some knowledge about a project remains relatively stable: its identity, its general positioning, and its intended segment rarely shift quickly. Other knowledge changes continuously: pricing, availability, and offers can shift at any time. MAYA distinguishes clearly between the two, and does not treat information from one category with the confidence appropriate to the other.

## Limits of Knowledge

Project knowledge does not claim to be complete. Where a piece of information is not available, that gap is acknowledged plainly. It is never filled with an invented substitute, regardless of how helpful a confident answer might seem in the moment.

## Relationship to the Other Minds

Project knowledge does not function on its own. It is always interpreted through the Mental Model, the Customer Model, the Knowledge Model, and the Project Brain it belongs to — the same facts about a project mean different things depending on the customer and situation they are being considered against.

## Definition of Success

The success of project knowledge is not measured by the number of documents behind it, nor by the length of a brochure. It is measured by its ability to give MAYA the understanding needed to offer a consultation that is accurate, honest, and genuinely fitting for each individual customer.
