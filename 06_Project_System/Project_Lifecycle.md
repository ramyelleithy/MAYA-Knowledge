# Project Lifecycle

## Philosophy

A project is not a fixed picture. It is not a set of details recorded once and left unchanged. Every project moves through stages, and at each one, the knowledge surrounding it changes, priorities change, and even the way the project is presented to customers may change. Understanding a project, therefore, means understanding its current position within its lifecycle. This document extends `Project_Brain.md`, `Project_Knowledge.md`, and `Knowledge_Evolution.md` directly: it describes the dimension of time that runs through a project's identity, not a construction schedule or a development roadmap.

## What Project Lifecycle Is

A project's lifecycle is its development from the moment it emerges until its role in the market has run its course. It is not limited to construction stages — it spans the project's entire life as something MAYA holds knowledge of and reasons about.

## The Project Changes

A project's information, features, priorities, target audience, and competitive value can all shift over time. None of these is assumed to remain as it was when the project was first understood — a project genuinely becomes a different thing to know as it moves through its lifecycle.

## Knowledge Is Tied to Stage

The same piece of information can carry different weight depending on the stage a project is currently in. For this reason, knowledge about a project cannot be separated from its timing — a fact that mattered greatly at one stage may matter far less at another, and treating it as timeless would misrepresent it.

## Value Changes

A project's consultative value is not fixed. A project can become more suitable, or less, because of shifts in the market or in the project itself. What made a project the right recommendation at one point does not guarantee it remains the right recommendation later.

## Handling Completed Projects

A project reaching the end of its sales cycle, or the completion of its construction, does not mean its knowledge value disappears. It can remain important for comparison, for analysis, for resale, and for understanding market behavior — a completed project still has a place in MAYA's understanding, even once it is no longer being actively offered.

## Relationship to the Market

A project does not change in isolation from its surrounding environment. It is affected by competition, demand, regulation, the economy, and other external factors — its lifecycle is shaped as much by what surrounds it as by anything intrinsic to the project itself.

## Continuity of Identity

A project may change considerably, yet it retains a core identity that must be preserved within MAYA's knowledge of it. What shifts across a lifecycle is the project's circumstances and standing; what does not shift is what fundamentally makes it recognizably itself.

## Consultation Across Time

MAYA does not offer the same consultation about a project at every point in time. She adapts her consultation according to the project's current stage — the same underlying project can rightly be discussed very differently depending on where it stands.

## Relationship to the Rest of the System

Project lifecycle works in integration with Project Brain, Project Knowledge, Knowledge Evolution, and Knowledge Sources, giving MAYA a temporal understanding of a project rather than a static description of it. It is the dimension that keeps everything else about a project honest to where it currently stands.

## Definition of Success

The success of understanding a project's lifecycle is not measured by the number of stages defined, nor by how detailed they are. It is measured by MAYA's ability to understand a project in its actual temporal context, and to offer a consultation that reflects its present reality rather than an outdated picture of it.
